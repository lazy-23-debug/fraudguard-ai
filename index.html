SHAP Explainability Analysis

SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations) assigns each feature a contribution value for every fraud prediction. This satisfies Explainability Design requirements under Design Science Research — making the Hybrid Framework's decisions auditable for fraud analysts and regulators, addressing a key adoption barrier identified in Diffusion of Innovation Theory.

Global Feature Importance — Mean |SHAP| Values (ULB Dataset)
Feature Direction — Fraud vs. Legitimate
01
V14 — Most Discriminative Feature

V14 (a PCA component from the original data) holds the highest mean absolute SHAP value. In published analyses of the ULB dataset, extreme negative values of V14 are strongly predictive of fraud — suggesting this component captures unusual authorization or authentication context in the original transaction metadata that fraudsters cannot easily mimic.

02
V10 — Second Highest Impact

V10 interacts strongly with V14 to create compound discrimination between fraudulent and legitimate high-amount transactions. Together, V14 and V10 account for over 40% of the total SHAP contribution pool in fraud-positive cases.

03
Amount — Only Interpretable Feature

Transaction Amount is the only semantically interpretable feature in the ULB dataset. Fraud cases show a bimodal distribution: very small probe transactions (testing card validity) and very large extraction transactions. This bimodal pattern gives Amount significant predictive power at both tails.

04
V4 & V11 — Behavioral Signals

V4 and V11 capture cardholder behavioral patterns — likely spending frequency, merchant category patterns, or geographic signals in the original pre-PCA data. Their combination with Amount creates strong discrimination for high-value fraudulent NIP-like transfers.

05
V17 & V12 — Temporal Signatures

V17 and V12 are moderate-impact contributors that likely encode temporal or sequential patterns — the time elapsed since account activity or the pattern of recent transactions. These features inform the Autoencoder's reconstruction error signal more than XGBoost's direct classification.

06
V31 — Autoencoder Reconstruction Error

V31 is the engineered feature unique to this Hybrid Framework: the Autoencoder's reconstruction error score, appended as an additional input to XGBoost. Its high SHAP ranking confirms that the anomaly signal from the unsupervised component provides genuine discriminative value beyond what the original 30 features alone achieve.

FraudGuard AI

Hybrid ML Framework for Fraud Detection in Nigerian Digital Payment Systems

This web application demonstrates a stacking ensemble architecture that integrates an Autoencoder (unsupervised anomaly detector) with XGBoost (gradient-boosted supervised classifier), achieving state-of-the-art fraud detection performance with full SHAP-based explainability. Built as a final-year research project at Caleb University, it addresses the ₦52.26 billion fraud problem in Nigerian digital payments (NIBSS, 2025).

Python 3.11 TensorFlow / Keras XGBoost scikit-learn imbalanced-learn (SMOTE) SHAP FastAPI Pandas / NumPy
